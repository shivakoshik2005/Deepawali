The Triumph of Light

Deepawali, the "Festival of Lights," celebrates the victory of good over evil, light over darkness, and knowledge over ignorance.

One cherished tradition associates Diwali with the return of Lord Rama to Ayodhya after defeating the demon king Ravana. To honor his return, the people illuminated the kingdom with rows of clay lamps (diyas), symbolizing the triumph of light.

Another significant legend is that of Lord Krishna and his consort Satyabhama vanquishing the demon Narakasura, freeing the world from his tyranny. Their joyous return was marked by grand celebrations, with people lighting lamps and bursting firecrackers, signifying liberation from darkness. These stories beautifully encapsulate the spirit of Deepawali.
